Panchakarma

Panchakarma in Ayurveda literally means five therapeutic ways of treatment to eliminate toxins from the body and to replenish the tissues of the patient.

Panchakarma is normally performed in three different stages:

  • Poorvakarma - Preparatory procedure that prepares the body for discarding toxins
  • Pradhanakarma - Main Panchakarma procedure
  • Paschatkarma - Post-treatment care

Poorvakarma helps to liquefy the vitiated doshas and brings the same to koshtas (alimentary canal) from where the toxins are removed by panchakarma processes.

Panchakarma procedures include:

  • VAMANAM (Emesis)
  • VIRECHANAM (Purgation)
  • SNEHAVASTHI (Medicated oil enema)
  • KASHAYAVASTHI (Enema with herbal decoction, oils, honey etc)
  • NASYAM (Instillation of medicines through nose)

The person seeking Ayurvedic treatment is assessed for his/her physical and mental requirements and treatment is prescribed for various purposes like curative treatments, disease prevention, rejuvenation and revitalization.

The diagnostic methods in ayurveda are interrogation, pulse diagnosis and physical examination. If you are under any treatment or if you have done any laboratory test, it is always advisable to inform the doctor in the first consultation itself.

The herbal oils and powders used for Panchakarma are produced in our own pharmacy to ensure quality and effectiveness of treatment.
